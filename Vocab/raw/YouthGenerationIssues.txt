Dưới đây là danh sách đầy đủ 200 từ vựng cho chủ đề **Youth & Generation Issues** (Thế hệ trẻ và các vấn đề thế hệ), được thiết kế theo đúng định dạng trải phẳng (flat) chi tiết nhất, bao gồm: **Từ vựng (Từ loại) /Phiên âm/: Nghĩa tiếng Anh (Giải thích chi tiết nghĩa tiếng Việt) + Ví dụ minh họa.**

---

1. **Adolescence (n) /ˌædəˈlesns/:** The period following the onset of puberty during which a young person develops from a child into an adult. (Giai đoạn thanh thiếu niên từ trẻ con sang người lớn.).
Ví dụ: Adolescence is a turbulent time of life for many teenagers.

---

2. **Puberty (n) /ˈpjuːbərti/:** The period during which adolescents reach sexual maturity and become capable of reproduction. (Giai đoạn dậy thì của trẻ vị thành niên.).
Ví dụ: Most girls reach puberty between the ages of 10 and 14.

---

3. **Generation Gap (n) /ˌdʒenəˈreɪʃn ɡæp/:** A difference of opinions between one generation and another regarding beliefs, politics, or values. (Khoảng cách thế hệ về quan điểm và tư duy.).
Ví dụ: The generation gap often leads to misunderstandings between parents and children.

---

4. **Peer pressure (n) /ˈpɪər preʃər/:** Influence from members of one's peer group to behave in a manner similar to them. (Áp lực phải làm theo hoặc giống với bạn bè đồng trang lứa.).
Ví dụ: Many teenagers start smoking due to peer pressure.

---

5. **Juvenile (adj) /ˈdʒuːvənl/:** For or relating to young people who are not yet adults. (Liên quan đến người vị thành niên, thường dùng trong ngữ cảnh pháp luật.).
Ví dụ: The town has seen a rise in juvenile crime over the past year.

---

6. **Delinquency (n) /dɪˈlɪŋkwənsi/:** Minor crime, especially that committed by young people. (Sự phạm pháp hoặc hành vi sai trái của người trẻ tuổi.).
Ví dụ: Poverty is often linked to high rates of juvenile delinquency.

---

7. **Socialization (n) /ˌsoʊʃələˈzeɪʃn/:** The process of learning to behave in a way that is acceptable to society. (Quá trình hòa nhập và học hỏi các quy tắc xã hội.).
Ví dụ: Schools play a vital role in the socialization of children.

---

8. **Alienation (n) /ˌeɪliəˈneɪʃn/:** The state or experience of being isolated from a group or an activity to which one should belong. (Cảm giác bị cô lập hoặc xa lánh khỏi cộng đồng.).
Ví dụ: Unemployment can lead to a sense of social alienation among young people.

---

9. **Rebellion (n) /rɪˈbeljən/:** An act of open resistance to an established government, ruler, or parental authority. (Sự nổi loạn hoặc chống đối lại các quy tắc/cha mẹ.).
Ví dụ: Teenagers often go through a period of rebellion against their parents' rules.

---

10. **Conformity (n) /kənˈfɔːrməti/:** Behavior in accordance with socially accepted standards or conventions. (Sự tuân thủ theo các chuẩn mực và kỳ vọng của xã hội.).
Ví dụ: There is a lot of pressure for conformity in high school.

---

11. **Identity crisis (n) /aɪˈdentəti ˌkraɪsɪs/:** A period of uncertainty and confusion in which a person's sense of identity becomes insecure. (Khủng hoảng danh tính, không rõ mình là ai.).
Ví dụ: Many adolescents experience an identity crisis as they try to find their place in the world.

---

12. **Self-esteem (n) /ˌself ɪˈstiːm/:** Confidence in one's own worth or abilities; self-respect. (Lòng tự trọng hoặc sự tự tin vào giá trị của bản thân.).
Ví dụ: Low self-esteem can prevent young people from pursuing their dreams.

---

13. **Narcissism (n) /ˈnɑːrsɪsɪzəm/:** Excessive interest in or admiration of oneself and one's physical appearance. (Sự tự luyến, quá yêu bản thân và khao khát sự chú ý.).
Ví dụ: Some critics argue that social media encourages narcissism in Gen Z.

---

14. **Millennials (n) /mɪˈleniəlz/:** People reaching young adulthood in the early 21st century, typically born between 1981 and 1996. (Thế hệ Millennials, hay thế hệ Y.).
Ví dụ: Millennials are known for being the first generation to grow up with the internet.

---

15. **Gen Z (n) /ˌdʒen ˈziː/:** The generation born between the late 1990s and the early 2010s. (Thế hệ Z, lớn lên trong kỷ nguyên số toàn diện.).
Ví dụ: Gen Z is often characterized by its digital fluency and social activism.

---

16. **Digital natives (n) /ˌdɪdʒɪtl ˈneɪtɪvz/:** People born or brought up during the age of digital technology and therefore familiar with it from early childhood. (Người bản địa số.).
Ví dụ: Unlike their parents, Gen Z are true digital natives.

---

17. **Cyberbullying (n) /ˈsaɪbərbʊliɪŋ/:** The use of electronic communication to bully a person, typically by sending messages of an intimidating nature. (Bắt nạt qua mạng.).
Ví dụ: Schools are taking measures to educate students about the dangers of cyberbullying.

---

18. **Screen addiction (n) /ˈskriːn əˌdɪkʃn/:** The compulsive use of digital devices such as smartphones or computers. (Chứng nghiện sử dụng điện thoại và các thiết bị điện tử.).
Ví dụ: Screen addiction can lead to sleep deprivation and poor academic performance.

---

19. **Career orientation (n) /kəˈrɪr ˌɔːriənˈteɪʃn/:** The process of assisting young people in choosing and preparing for a profession. (Hoạt động định hướng nghề nghiệp.).
Ví dụ: Good career orientation in high school helps students make better college choices.

---

20. **Job insecurity (n) /ˌdʒɑːb ˌɪnsɪˈkjʊrəti/:** The state of knowing that your job is not permanent or may be lost at any time. (Sự bấp bênh và thiếu ổn định trong công việc.).
Ví dụ: Many young graduates face job insecurity in today's competitive market.

---

21. **Underemployment (n) /ˌʌndərɪmˈplɔɪmənt/:** The condition of being employed at a job that is inadequate to one's training. (Tình trạng làm việc dưới năng lực.).
Ví dụ: Underemployment is a serious issue for many highly educated young people.

---

22. **Gig economy (n) /ˈɡɪɡ ɪˌkɑːnəmi/:** A labor market characterized by short-term contracts or freelance work. (Nền kinh tế việc làm tự do hoặc thời vụ.).
Ví dụ: The gig economy offers flexibility but lacks the benefits of a stable job.

---

23. **Student debt (n) /ˈstjuːdnt det/:** Financial obligations accumulated by students to cover the costs of their education. (Các khoản nợ vay để học đại học của sinh viên.).
Ví dụ: High levels of student debt are delaying major life milestones like buying a home.

---

24. **Academic pressure (n) /ˌækəˈdemɪk ˈpreʃər/:** Stress and anxiety resulting from the high demands of school performance. (Áp lực học hành thi cử.).
Ví dụ: Excessive academic pressure can lead to mental health problems in teenagers.

---

25. **Extracurricular (adj) /ˌekstrəkəˈrɪkjələr/:** An activity at a school or college pursued in addition to the normal course of study. (Hoạt động ngoại khóa.).

---

26. **Gap year (n) /ˈɡæp jɪər/:** A year taken by a student as a break between school and university. (Năm nghỉ để trải nghiệm trước khi vào đại học.).
Ví dụ: He decided to take a gap year to travel and volunteer in Africa.

---

27. **Volunteering (n) /ˌvɑːlənˈtɪrɪŋ/:** Freely offering to do something or help others, often for a community or organization. (Hoạt động tình nguyện.).
Ví dụ: Volunteering at a local hospital provides valuable experience for medical students.

---

28. **Soft skills (n) /ˈsɔːft skɪlz/:** Personal attributes that enable someone to interact effectively with others. (Kỹ năng mềm như giao tiếp, teamwork.).
Ví dụ: Employers value soft skills just as much as technical qualifications.

---

29. **Interpersonal (adj) /ˌɪntərˈpɜːrsənl/:** Relating to relationships or communication between people. (Liên quan đến giao tiếp cá nhân.).
Ví dụ: He has excellent interpersonal skills and gets along with everyone.

---

30. **Critical thinking (n) /ˌkrɪtɪkl ˈθɪŋkɪŋ/:** The objective analysis of an issue in order to form a judgment. (Tư duy phản biện.).
Ví dụ: The school curriculum aims to develop students' critical thinking abilities.

---

31. **Empathy (n) /ˈempəθi/:** The ability to understand and share the feelings of another person. (Sự thấu cảm.).
Ví dụ: Reading literature helps young people develop empathy for others.

---

32. **Mindfulness (n) /ˈmaɪndflnəs/:** A mental state achieved by focusing one's awareness on the present moment. (Sự chánh niệm.).
Ví dụ: Practicing mindfulness can help students manage exam-related stress.

---

33. **Mental health (n) /ˌmentl ˈhelθ/:** A person's condition with regard to their psychological and emotional well-being. (Sức khỏe tâm thần.).
Ví dụ: There is a growing awareness of the importance of mental health among youth.

---

34. **Burnout (n) /ˈbɜːrnaʊt/:** State of emotional, physical, and mental exhaustion caused by excessive stress. (Sự kiệt sức.).

---

35. **Depression (n) /dɪˈpreʃn/:** A serious medical condition in which a person feels very sad and hopeless. (Chứng bệnh trầm cảm.).
Ví dụ: It is important to seek professional help if you are suffering from depression.

36. **Anxiety (n) /æŋˈzaɪəti/:** A feeling of worry, nervousness, or unease about an uncertain outcome. (Sự lo âu.).

37. **Loneliness (n) /ˈloʊnlinəs/:** The sadness that is caused by having no friends or being by oneself. (Sự cô đơn.).

40. **Vaping (n) /ˈveɪpɪŋ/:** The action of inhaling and exhaling the vapor produced by an electronic cigarette. (Việc hút thuốc lá điện tử.).

41. **Obesity (n) /oʊˈbiːsəti/:** The state of being overweight to a degree that is unhealthy. (Bệnh béo phì.).

43. **Nutrition (n) /nuˈtrɪʃn/:** The process of obtaining the food necessary for health and growth. (Chế độ dinh dưỡng.).

51. **Climate anxiety (n) /ˈklaɪmət æŋˌzaɪəti/:** Worry about the future of the environment due to climate change. (Nỗi lo lắng về biến đổi khí hậu.).

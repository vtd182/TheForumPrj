### Housework and cooking

#### 1. Did you do some house cleaning when you were young?

**[Main ideas]{.cdblue}:**

- Helping with the upkeep of the home was a mandatory part of my childhood routine.
- I was generally tasked with \"small-scale\" chores like setting the table or sweeping.

Without a doubt, helping with the upkeep of the home was a mandatory part of my childhood routine. I was generally tasked with \"small-scale\" chores, such as setting the dinner table or sweeping the kitchen floor after meals. Looking back, while I might have complained about the manual labor back then, those early habits definitely instilled a sense of discipline and self-reliance in me.

#### 2. What housework do you like or dislike doing?

**[Main ideas]{.cdblue}:**

- I find laundry quite therapeutic, especially the process of folding clothes neatly.
- On the contrary, I have a strong aversion to washing dishes as it feels never-ending.
- I usually try to outsource the tasks I dislike to a robot vacuum to save time.

#### 3. Do you like cooking?

**[Main ideas]{.cdblue}:**

- I'd consider myself a bit of a \"foodie,\" so I love experimenting with exotic recipes.
- There is something incredibly rewarding about creating a nutritious meal from scratch.
- It serves as a creative outlet for me, away from the stresses of professional life.

#### 4. How often do you cook?

**[Main ideas]{.cdblue}:**

- I make a point of cooking on a daily basis to ensure I maintain a balanced diet.
- I'm a firm believer that home-cooked meals are far superior to processed alternatives.
- That said, I'll occasionally treat myself to a fancy dinner out if I'm feeling particularly lazy.

To be perfectly honest, free time is a bit of a luxury for me these days. My calendar is typically overflowing with various academic commitments and professional deadlines, leaving very little room for leisure. Consequently, I have to be quite intentional about carving out small pockets of time for self-care just to avoid burning out.

#### 2. What do you usually do in your spare time?

**[Main ideas]{.cdblue}:**

- I'm quite into creative pursuits, such as photography or sketching.
- It's a fantastic way to disconnect from the digital world and find my \"flow.\"
- Alternatively, I'm an avid reader and love getting lost in a good mystery novel.

#### 3. Do you have more free time on weekdays or at weekends?

**[Main ideas]{.cdblue}:**

- Without question, the weekends offer much more flexibility.
- Weekdays are characterized by a rigid routine of commuting and working.
- The weekend provides a much-needed break from the \"hustle and bustle\" of city life.

#### 4. Which day do you have more free time on, Saturday or Sunday?

**[Main ideas]{.cdblue}:**

- Sunday is definitely the winner in terms of leisure time.
- I tend to front-load my chores on Saturday to keep the following day completely clear.
- Sunday is my designated \"do-nothing\" day, which helps me mentally prepare for the week ahead.

## PART 3

#### 1. How are the transportation systems in urban areas and rural areas different?

The discrepancy between these two settings is primarily defined by accessibility and infrastructure investment. Urban centers typically boast sophisticated, multi-modal transit systems designed to facilitate the rapid movement of large populations. Conversely, rural transportation is often characterized by its scarcity, leaving inhabitants largely dependent on personal automobiles due to the lack of viable public alternatives. Moreover, the sheer density of traffic in cities necessitates complex management systems, whereas rural transit is more localized and less regulated, reflecting the different economic priorities of each region.

#### 2. Do you think people need to change the way of transportation drastically to protect the environment?

Absolutely. The current trajectory of global transport is largely unsustainable, given its heavy dependence on carbon-intensive fuels. A drastic transformation is imperative, involving not only the adoption of electric and hydrogen-powered vehicles but also a fundamental shift in urban design to prioritize \"active transport\" like cycling. Furthermore, incentivizing long-distance rail over domestic flights could significantly curtail greenhouse gas emissions. Without such a comprehensive overhaul of our transit habits, achieving meaningful climate targets will remain an elusive goal for the international community.

#### 3. Do you think air pollution comes mostly from mobile vehicles?

It is widely acknowledged that vehicular emissions are a predominant factor in urban air degradation; however, the reality is more nuanced. While the internal combustion engine is responsible for a vast array of particulate matter and nitrogen oxides, industrial manufacturing and energy production are equally culpable. In many developing nations, domestic biomass burning and construction dust also exacerbate the issue significantly. Thus, while targeting mobile vehicles is a crucial step in improving air quality, it must be part of a broader strategy that addresses industrial regulation.

### Housework and cooking

#### 1. Did you do some house cleaning when you were young?

- My parents were quite strict, so I had to help with chores regularly.
- My main responsibility was tidying up the living room and dusting the furniture.

Actually, my parents were quite strict, so I had to help with chores regularly starting from a young age. My main responsibility was tidying up the living room and dusting the furniture before guests arrived. To be fair, I found it a bit annoying at the time, but looking back, I think it taught me how to be more organized and responsible.

**[Main ideas]{.cdblue}:**

- I actually enjoy organizing my bookshelf or wardrobe because it feels productive.
- I can\'t stand scrubbing the bathroom as it is quite exhausting.
- I prefer tasks that I can do while listening to music or a podcast.

- I'm quite fond of cooking because it allows me to control what I eat.
- It is a great way to relieve stress after a long day at work or school.
- However, I find the cleaning process after cooking to be quite a hassle.

## PART 2

::: prompt
**Describe a bicycle/motorcycle/car trip you would like to go**
- Who you would like to go with
- Where you would like to go
- When you would like to go
- And explain why you would like to go by bicycle/motorcycle/car
:::

**Sample answer:**

I'd like to describe an adventurous motorcycle trip I've been dreaming of. I'd ideally go with a small group of close friends who share my passion for exploring off-the-beaten-path locations. We would head towards the scenic highlands, specifically targeting the winding coastal roads before climbing up to the mountains. I'm planning to do this around mid-March because the climate is typically mild and pleasant, which is perfect for long-distance riding.

The reason I'd opt for a motorcycle over a car is primarily for the sense of freedom it provides. Traveling on two wheels allows you to feel much more connected to the environment; you can truly appreciate the shifting landscapes and the cool mountain breeze. Furthermore, motorcycles offer incredible flexibility. If we spot a hidden cafe or a breathtaking viewpoint, we can simply pull over without worrying about parking space. Although it might be more physically demanding than driving a car, the sense of accomplishment you get from navigating those mountain passes is unparalleled. It would be a fantastic opportunity to bond with my friends while escaping the hustle and bustle of city life.

## PART 3

#### 1. How are the transportation systems in urban areas and rural areas different?

The disparity between urban and rural transportation is quite significant. In metropolitan areas, the infrastructure is highly developed, featuring extensive networks of buses and subways that offer frequent service. In contrast, rural areas often suffer from a lack of public facilities, forcing residents to rely heavily on private vehicles. Furthermore, while city roads are generally well-maintained to handle heavy traffic, countryside paths may be underdeveloped. Consequently, urban dwellers enjoy much higher mobility and more diverse commuting options than those living in remote regions.

#### 2. Do you think people need to change the way of transportation drastically to protect the environment?

I definitely believe a radical shift in our transportation habits is necessary to safeguard the environment. Currently, the over-reliance on fossil-fuel-powered vehicles is a primary driver of global warming. To mitigate this, we need to transition towards greener alternatives, such as electric cars and high-speed rail systems. Additionally, governments should encourage cycling and walking by improving urban planning. Without these drastic changes, it will be nearly impossible to reduce carbon footprints and prevent further ecological damage to our planet in the future.

#### 3. Do you think air pollution comes mostly from mobile vehicles?

While mobile vehicles are undoubtedly a major contributor to poor air quality, they aren\'t the sole culprit. In densely populated cities, exhaust fumes from heavy traffic create significant smog, which is detrimental to public health. However, we must also consider industrial activities and large-scale power plants that burn coal. These sources release massive amounts of pollutants into the atmosphere as well. Therefore, although transport emissions play a leading role in air pollution, a holistic approach is needed to address all industrial factors.

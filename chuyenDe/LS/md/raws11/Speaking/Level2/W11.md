## PART 2

::: prompt
**Describe a wild animal that you want to learn more about**
- What it is
- When/where you saw it
- Why you want to learn more about it
- And explain what you want to learn more about it
:::

**Sample answer:**

I'd like to describe a fascinating creature that has recently captured my curiosity: the snow leopard. I first encountered this animal through a stunning online photography exhibition a few months ago. I was immediately struck by its nickname, \"the ghost of the mountains,\" which refers to its elusive nature and how difficult it is to spot in the wild.

The reason I'm so keen to learn more about this predator is due to its remarkable adaptations. It lives in the rugged terrain of Central Asia, and I find it incredible how it can navigate steep, icy cliffs with such agility. Specifically, I'm interested in researching their hunting techniques and how they use their exceptionally long tails for balance. Furthermore, I'd like to explore the conservation efforts being made to protect them. Since they are a \"vulnerable\" species, I want to understand the impact of climate change on their natural habitat and what local communities are doing to prevent their extinction. To me, the snow leopard represents the mystery and beauty of the wilderness, and gaining more knowledge about them would be truly rewarding.

## PART 3

#### 1. Is it more important to protect wild animals or the environment?

In my view, protecting the environment should be the priority because it serves as the foundation for all life. If a habitat is destroyed by pollution or deforestation, the species living there will inevitably face extinction. By preserving ecosystems, we naturally safeguard the diverse creatures that inhabit them. While protecting specific animals is valuable, it is ultimately a temporary solution if their natural surroundings are not maintained. Therefore, environmental conservation provides a more sustainable, long-term benefit for the entire planet.

#### 2. Should people educate children to protect wild animals?

Definitely. It is crucial to instill a sense of environmental responsibility in the younger generation. By educating children about the importance of biodiversity, we encourage them to develop empathetic attitudes toward nature. This can be achieved through interactive school programs or outdoor activities that connect them with wildlife. When children understand the vital role that animals play in our ecosystem, they are much more likely to support conservation efforts and make eco-friendly choices as they transition into adulthood.

#### 3. Why do some people attach more importance to protecting rare animals?

Many individuals prioritize rare animals because their extinction represents an irreversible loss to our planet's biological diversity. These \"umbrella species\" often become symbols of conservation movements, capturing public imagination and sympathy more effectively than common animals. Furthermore, people tend to value things that are scarce. The thought of losing a unique species forever creates a sense of urgency, leading people to donate more money and effort toward saving these high-profile animals from the brink of total disappearance.
